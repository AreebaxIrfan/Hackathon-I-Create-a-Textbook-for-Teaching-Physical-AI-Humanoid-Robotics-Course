# Detailed Weekly Breakdown

This section provides a deeper dive into the topics covered each week, complementing the high-level overview in the Learning Outcomes. Each week balances theoretical concepts with practical application, culminating in hands-on labs and project work.

---

## Week 1: ROS 2 Fundamentals & Setup

*   **Topics**:
    *   Introduction to ROS 2 architecture: nodes, topics, services, actions, parameters.
    *   Setting up your development environment: Ubuntu, Docker, VS Code integration.
    *   Basic ROS 2 command-line tools (`ros2 run`, `ros2 topic list`, `ros2 node info`).
    *   Creating your first ROS 2 package: structure, `package.xml`, `CMakeLists.txt`.
    *   Publishing and subscribing to topics: "Hello World" of ROS 2 communication.
*   **Lab Focus**: Bring up a basic ROS 2 network, publish sensor data, subscribe to control commands.

---

## Week 2: Advanced ROS 2 & System Integration

*   **Topics**:
    *   ROS 2 Services: Request/response communication patterns.
    *   ROS 2 Actions: Long-running, preemptable tasks.
    *   Transformations (`tf2`): Understanding coordinate frames and kinematic trees.
    *   ROS 2 Launch system: Orchestrating multiple nodes and configurations.
    *   Parameter management: Dynamic configuration of nodes.
*   **Lab Focus**: Build a multi-node ROS 2 application for a simple robot, involving movement commands and feedback.

---

## Week 3: Introduction to Robotic Simulation & Gazebo

*   **Topics**:
    *   Importance of simulation in robotics: rapid prototyping, safe testing, data generation.
    *   Overview of Gazebo and its integration with ROS 2.
    *   Understanding URDF (Unified Robot Description Format) for robot modeling.
    *   Creating and importing simple robot models into Gazebo.
    *   Controlling simulated robots via ROS 2 interfaces.
*   **Lab Focus**: Spawn a pre-built robot model in Gazebo and teleoperate it using ROS 2.

---

## Week 4: Advanced Simulation & Unity Integration

*   **Topics**:
    *   NVIDIA Isaac Sim: Introduction and capabilities on Omniverse.
    *   Comparing Gazebo and Isaac Sim: strengths, weaknesses, use cases.
    *   Introduction to Unity for robotics simulation (Unity Robotics Hub).
    *   Designing complex environments and physics interactions.
    *   Data generation from simulation for AI training.
*   **Lab Focus**: Transfer a robot model to Isaac Sim or Unity, perform advanced manipulation tasks in simulation.

---

## Week 5: NVIDIA Isaac ROS & AI Perception

*   **Topics**:
    *   Overview of the NVIDIA Isaac ecosystem: hardware, software, cloud.
    *   Isaac ROS: Accelerated perception algorithms (image processing, object detection, segmentation).
    *   Deep learning integration with ROS 2: leveraging GPUs for real-time inference.
    *   Sensor fusion: combining camera, LiDAR, and IMU data for robust perception.
*   **Lab Focus**: Implement a real-time object detection pipeline on a simulated robot using Isaac ROS.

---

## Week 6: Isaac ROS Navigation & Manipulation

*   **Topics**:
    *   Isaac ROS navigation stack: SLAM, path planning, obstacle avoidance.
    *   Motion planning and inverse kinematics for robotic arms.
    *   Task planning and execution: integrating perception with action.
    *   Reinforcement Learning (RL) for robot control in Isaac Sim.
*   **Lab Focus**: Develop a navigation and manipulation pipeline for a simulated humanoid robot to interact with its environment.

---

## Week 7: Foundations of Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Models

*   **Topics**:
    *   Introduction to multimodal AI: combining vision and language.
    *   Large Language Models (LLMs) and their application in robotics.
    *   Vision Transformers (ViT) and their role in visual understanding.
    *   Bridging language commands to robot actions: task grounding.
*   **Lab Focus**: Experiment with an open-source VLA model to translate simple language commands into abstract robot actions.

---

## Week 8: Advanced VLA & Human-Robot Interaction

*   **Topics**:
    *   Fine-tuning VLA models for specific robotic tasks.
    *   Learning from demonstration and human feedback.
    *   Human-robot collaboration: safety, trust, communication.
    *   Ethical considerations in VLA-powered robotics.
*   **Lab Focus**: Enhance a VLA model to understand more complex, ambiguous human instructions and execute a multi-step task.

---

## Week 9: Capstone Project - Ideation & Planning

*   **Topics**:
    *   Review of all curriculum modules.
    *   Brainstorming and defining capstone project ideas.
    *   Project proposal development: problem statement, objectives, methodology.
    *   Setting up development environment for capstone project.
*   **Lab Focus**: Formalize capstone project scope and create an initial design document.

---

## Week 10: Capstone Project - Implementation & Integration

*   **Topics**:
    *   Implementing core functionalities of the capstone project.
    *   Integrating ROS 2, simulation tools, and AI components.
    *   Troubleshooting common integration challenges.
    *   Version control and collaborative development practices.
*   **Lab Focus**: Build and integrate major components of your capstone project in a simulated environment.

---

## Week 11: Capstone Project - Testing & Refinement

*   **Topics**:
    *   Testing methodologies for robotic systems: unit, integration, system tests.
    *   Debugging strategies for complex AI-robot systems.
    *   Performance optimization and resource management.
    *   Preparing for final demonstration and presentation.
*   **Lab Focus**: Rigorously test your capstone project, identify and fix bugs, and refine robot behavior.

---

## Week 12: Capstone Project - Demonstration & Presentation

*   **Topics**:
    *   Effective technical communication: presenting complex projects.
    *   Showcasing autonomous humanoid capabilities.
    *   Peer review and feedback sessions.
*   **Lab Focus**: Final demonstration of your autonomous humanoid robot project and presentation of findings.

---

## Week 13: Advanced Topics & Future Directions

*   **Topics**:
    *   Emerging trends in Physical AI and humanoid robotics.
    *   Research challenges and open problems.
    *   Career paths and opportunities in advanced robotics.
    *   Review of individual capstone projects and lessons learned.
*   **Lab Focus**: Exploration of a cutting-edge robotics paper or technology based on individual interest.

---
This detailed weekly breakdown serves as a comprehensive guide, ensuring students acquire a deep and practical understanding of physical AI and humanoid robotics.
